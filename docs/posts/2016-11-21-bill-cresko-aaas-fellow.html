November 21, 2016

We are thrilled to announce that Dr. William Cresko has been elected as a Fellow of the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS)!

This prestigious honor recognizes Bill’s distinguished contributions to evolutionary genetics and genomics, particularly his groundbreaking work on the genomic basis of evolution in threespine stickleback fish. AAAS Fellowship is among the highest distinctions in American science.

AAAS Fellowship Significance

This honor represents:

  • Recognition by the world’s largest scientific society
  • Distinguished contributions to science
  • Advancing science to serve society
  • Leadership in the scientific community
  • Lifetime achievement recognition

Research Contributions

Bill was recognized for:

  • Pioneering work in evolutionary genomics
  • Development of RAD-seq methodology
  • Threespine stickleback as a model system
  • Understanding rapid evolution
  • Genomic basis of adaptation

Scientific Impact

Bill’s work has advanced:

  • Population genomics field
  • Next-generation sequencing applications
  • Evolutionary biology understanding
  • Model organism development
  • Genomic technology innovation

Leadership Roles

Beyond research, Bill serves as:

  • Lorry Lokey Chair in Biology
  • Associate Vice President of Research
  • Director of Genomics facilities
  • Mentor to numerous students
  • Scientific community leader

Innovation in Methods

Key methodological contributions:

  • Co-development of RAD-seq
  • Genomic mapping techniques
  • Population genomics pipelines
  • Open-source software development
  • Method dissemination through workshops

Educational Impact

Bill’s teaching and mentoring:

  • Trained dozens of students
  • Developed genomics curricula
  • International workshop instruction
  • Public science communication
  • Next-generation scientist development

Collaborative Science

Fellowship recognizes:

  • Building research networks
  • International collaborations
  • Interdisciplinary approaches
  • Team science leadership
  • Community resource development

University Recognition

This honor brings:

  • Prestige to UO Biology
  • Recognition of research excellence
  • Visibility for Oregon science
  • Inspiration for students
  • Institutional achievement

Selection Process

AAAS Fellowship involves:

  • Nomination by current Fellows
  • Rigorous peer review
  • Council approval
  • Recognition of excellence
  • Distinguished career achievements

Previous Lab Honors

This adds to lab recognitions:

  • NSF CAREER Award

Ceremony and Induction

Bill will be:

  • Presented with certificate and pin
  • Celebrated by scientific community
  • Featured in Science magazine

Continuing Excellence

This recognition motivates:

Congratulations Bill on this well-deserved recognition of your outstanding contributions to science!
